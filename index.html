About Me

I'm drawn to financial markets because they represent a fascinating intersection of rigorous mathematics, messy real-world data, and human decision-making. While predicting market movements is deceptively hard—even well-resourced firms struggle to beat broad benchmarks—recent advances in AI and machine learning have opened new possibilities for solving these problems with greater precision.

My background combines technical depth with practical problem-solving. I'm completing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with a Certificate in Data Intensive Computing at the University at Buffalo, with coursework in machine learning, statistical analytics, systems programming, and data-intensive computing. This foundation has equipped me to design models that leverage data, adapt to dynamic environments, and scale to handle large, time-sensitive datasets.

In my research and project work, I've applied these skills to real challenges. In my baseball analytics project (BetGenie), I designed a backtesting agent inspired by quantitative trading environments to evaluate feature combinations and surface composite metrics that improved predictive accuracy while reducing overfitting. In my infant affective computing research, I use large language models to filter and evaluate research literature at scale. Through virtual internships with JPMorgan Chase and Tata Group, I've gained exposure to financial systems, risk frameworks, and data-driven decision making.

My career goal is to become a Quant Data Engineer—working at the intersection of AI and finance to find patterns, solve problems, and build systems that navigate uncertainty. I'm committed to translating algorithms into real-world market applications while maintaining deep understanding of market structure, risk, and practical model performance.
